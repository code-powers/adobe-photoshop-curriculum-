Copyright = Legal protection for creative work. Think of it like a "Do Not Touch" sign on someone's art.

The Rule: If someone created it, they own it. You can't use it without permission (unless it's free or you buy a license).
üö® Reality Check: Using the wrong image can cost $150,000 in fines! That's not a joke - it's real lawsuits.

What's Protected by Copyright:

  • Photos (even phone pics)
  • Artwork and illustrations
  • Logo designs
  • Music and videos

Model Releases - When You Need Permission from People
15 POINTS
‚è∞ 7 MINUTES
üìö LEARN: Model & Location Releases

Even if you legally own a photo, you might still need permission from people or property owners!

Model Release: Written permission from people in photos to use their image for business purposes.
You NEED a model release when:
  • Person's face is clearly recognizable
  • Using photo for advertising/marketing
  • Person is the main subject
  • Photo implies they endorse your product
Examples:
‚úÖ Need Release: Photo of person holding your client's product
‚ùå Don't Need: Crowd photo where no face is recognizable
